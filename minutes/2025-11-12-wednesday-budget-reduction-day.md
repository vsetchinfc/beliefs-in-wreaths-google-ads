## Key Learnings - How Google Ads Bidding Works

**User Questions Answered:**

1. **"Does Google charge per impression or per click?"**
   - **Per click only.** Impressions (ad shows) are FREE.
   - Example: 16 impressions today = $0 cost, 5 clicks = $34.19 cost

2. **"Does higher CPC make ads more attractive to Google?"**
   - **YES!** Higher bid √ó Quality Score = Higher Ad Rank = More impressions (free exposure)
   - Google runs an auction for each search - higher bids win more auctions

3. **"Why raise bids at lower budget?"**
   - At $30/day budget with $6-7 bids, you get:
     - MORE impressions (maybe 10-20/day instead of 4)
     - Same or slightly more clicks (3-5/day)
     - Better ad positions (top of page)
     - Budget still controls total daily spend (Google stops at $30)
   - Higher bids DON'T increase daily spend (budget caps it), they increase impression SHARE

4. **"Should we pause morning traffic and only run afternoon/evening?"**
   - **NO** - Sample size too small (only 1 day)
   - Morning actually had BETTER CTR (50% vs 25%)
   - With only 16 impressions/day total, can't afford to lose any opportunities
   - CTR doesn't equal conversions - need 7-10 days of conversion data by hour first

## Phrase Match Behavior Clarified

**"Added" in Search Terms Report = Confusing Label**

Search terms showing "Added" status does NOT mean they were added as keywords. It means:
- "Added" = This search term matched an existing keyword you already have
- "None" = New search term Google hasn't categorized yet
- "Excluded" = It's a negative keyword

**Example from today:**
- "christmas wreath" showed as "Added" but actual "christmas wreath" broad match keyword is PAUSED
- These generic terms are triggering as close variants of "stylish christmas wreaths" phrase match
- This is normal phrase match behavior - keyword catches related searches naturally
